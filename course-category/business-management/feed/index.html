This Course is SBL KHAS Claimable for Employers Registered with HRD Corp.

DESCRIPTION

This 2-day workshop is designed for new or current software developers transitioning to a project management role. Participants will learn about project management stages, methodologies, stakeholder management, project scope, and project closure techniques. They will also learn how to create a project charter and risk register, assign tasks using the Power Matrix, use project management software tools, and apply the Scrum methodology. Assessment criteria include preparing a project charter and risk register, planning and assigning tasks, using project management software, writing user stories, and applying Scrum to a project.

WHO IS THIS FOR

This course is designed for two groups of people:

  • New hires or existing employees who have been assigned to lead or manage a new project in software development, and Software developers who are interested in transitioning into a project management role.
  • It is suitable for individuals who have some knowledge and experience in software development and want to gain a better understanding of project management concepts and tools.

WHAT WILL I LEARN

  • Welcome
  • Module 1: Introduction to Project Management
  • Module 2: Stakeholder Management
  • Module 3: Project Initiation
  • Module 4: Project Planning
  • Module 5: Project Execution using Scrum Methodology
  • Module 6: The Role of Project Management Software
  • Module 7: Project Simulator
  • Module 8: Managing Costs and Project Closure
  • QA & Conclusion

This Course is SBL KHAS Claimable for Employers Registered with HRD Corp.

DESCRIPTION

This comprehensive 2-day workshop provides participants with the essential knowledge and skills necessary for effective business process management. Participants will learn to identify, analyze, and improve business processes to increase efficiency, reduce waste, and achieve organizational goals. The course covers topics such as process flow charts, stakeholder management, probabilistic modeling, root cause analysis, process redesign principles, and more. This course is ideal for professionals involved in process management or those seeking to expand their knowledge in this area.

WHO IS THIS FOR

This course is ideal for professionals from a range of industries who are involved in process management or wish to gain an understanding of business processes. This may include project managers, business analysts, process engineers, operations managers, quality assurance professionals, and consultants. The course is also suitable for individuals who are interested in pursuing a career in process management or wish to expand their knowledge in this area. Participants should have a basic understanding of business processes and terminology.

  • Module 1: Introduction to Business Processes
